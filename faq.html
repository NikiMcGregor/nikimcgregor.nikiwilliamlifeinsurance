Frequently Asked Questions

Plain-English answers about term life insurance, the death benefit, group life insurance, and how to choose the right life insurance coverage.

What is a death benefit?
The death benefit is the amount a policy may pay to your beneficiaries if the insured person passes away while the policy is in force, subject to policy terms and claim approval by the carrier.
How does term life insurance work?
With term life insurance, you choose a term length (e.g., 10–30 years) and a coverage amount. If the insured dies during the term, the beneficiary may receive the death benefit according to the policy provisions.
Is group life insurance through my employer enough?
Group life insurance can be a great start, but amounts are often limited and may not be portable if you change jobs. Many families supplement with an individual policy to lock in coverage that fits long-term goals.
What’s a mutual insurance company?
A mutual insurance company is owned by policyholders rather than stockholders. That structure can influence dividends and long-term priorities. We can compare mutual and stock carriers to help you decide.
How much life insurance coverage do I need?
It depends on income, debts, dependents, and future plans. Common starting points are 10–12× annual income or a needs analysis that considers mortgage, childcare, and college costs. We’ll help tailor an amount for your situation.
Will I need a medical exam?
It varies by product and carrier. Some offer simplified or accelerated underwriting (no exam) for eligible applicants. Final approval and requirements are determined by the issuing life insurance company.
How do quotes and rates work?
Online quotes are estimates. Final rates depend on underwriting, health history, lifestyle, and other factors. Product availability and features vary by state and carrier.
